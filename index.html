Co-Founders

Meet the visionaries behind ADI.

Melissa M. Young

Co-Founder & Chief Artistic Officer

Melissa M. Young is a distinguished artistic leader and educator whose three-decade career blends visionary creativity, strategic leadership, and a deep commitment to advancing the performing arts. A Honduran American from Santa Ana, California, she trained at The Ailey School and earned graduate-level certificates in Leadership, Management, and Analytical Skills from Michigan State University's Eli Broad College of Business. She spent 30 transformative years with Dallas Black Dance Theatre, rising from company dancer to Artistic Director. She pioneered virtual programming connecting audiences across 40+ states and 35+ countries. Her artistic influence has appeared at The Kennedy Center, Lincoln Center, City Center, and The Joyce Theater. Today she also leads The Presence Whisperer and collaborates with the Serendipity Institute for Black Arts and Heritage.

Sam Santiago, CDE®

Co-Founder & Chief Executive Officer

Sam Santiago is a seasoned corporate executive and international entrepreneur with 35 years of experience across travel, tourism, hospitality, logistics, entertainment, and global trade associations. A native of Puerto Rico, Sam holds education from the Universidad de Puerto Rico and is a Certified Diversity Executive®. He served as leader of the American Airlines Foundation, was appointed Chair of the City of Dallas Arts and Culture Advisory Commission by Mayor Eric Johnson in 2019, and contributed to the 2018 Dallas Cultural Plan. He now serves as Global Advisor for Global Chamber Mexico, President of the Asociación Internacional de Hoteles Boutique, and co-founder and CEO of ADI.
